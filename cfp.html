RENEW - Call for Papers

The RENEW’26 (Resilient and ENergy-Efficient netWorks) workshop invites high-quality research contributions at the intersection of network sustainability, energy efficiency, and network resilience. As modern networks evolve into highly softwarized, virtualized, and AI-driven infrastructures, a fundamental shift is required: energy efficiency and resilience must be co-designed rather than treated as independent objectives. RENEW’26 provides a dedicated forum for researchers and practitioners investigating innovative approaches that enable networks to operate sustainably while remaining robust against faults, attacks, and resource constraints.

The workshop will be co-located with IEEE WoWMoM 2026, a premier venue on wireless, mobile, and ubiquitous networking.

We welcome original contributions, position papers, and experimental studies on (but not limited to):

  • Energy-aware communication protocols
  • Carbon-aware routing, service placement, and orchestration
  • Energy-adaptive virtual network functions (VNFs)
  • Sustainable architectures for 5G/6G and IoT
  • Lifecycle carbon impact analysis and eco-metrics for networks
  • Fault tolerance in softwarized networks (SDN, NFV, cloud-native)
  • Resilience to hardware/software failures and dynamic resource variability
  • Robustness under adversarial conditions, denial-of-service, or anomalies
  • Recovery and self-healing mechanisms in distributed systems
  • Predictive energy management using AI/ML
  • AI-assisted orchestration for balancing performance, resilience, and energy use
  • Intelligent anomaly detection and proactive failure prediction
  • ML-driven adaptive resource allocation under energy constraints
  • Secure and sustainable IoT/edge/cloud computing
  • Emulation/virtualization environments, datasets, and benchmarks
  • Regulatory trends and incentives for sustainable networking
  • Cost–benefit analysis of energy-efficient and resilient designs
